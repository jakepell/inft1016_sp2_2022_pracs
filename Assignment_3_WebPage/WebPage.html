About Neuromorphic Computing

The human brain is the most complex computer known to humanity. Equipped with power efficient computation and continuous learning through billions of synapses and neurons, storing information in unique locations of the brain. The brain makes up approximately 2 percent of human body weight, yet contains vital functions and qualities that defining our humanity. Additionally, the brain has structural properties that enable intelligent processing capable of complex tasks. For these reasons, AI professionals are focusing on replicating the architecture of the brain to bring a similar level of intelligence to computers. Founder and CTO of BrainChip, Peter van der Made expresses, “The brain is the only example that we have of an intelligent system. People have been trying to ‘reinvent’ intelligence ever since the earliest days of computing over 70 years ago.” (van der Made, 2021). This emphasizes how significant the brain Is, consequently prompting professionals to develop such architecture for computing.

Unique Properties of Neuromorphic Computing

Conventional computers utilising the von Neumann architecture will remain widely used for general purpose tasks. Neuromorphic computing introduces unique features that will consequently see AI peruse this architecture for majority of its applications. AI algorithms that compute on such hardware, serve the complex demands that machine learning and deep learning require to perform efficiently and effectively. Further discussed in a scientific report, “This ‘von Neumann bottleneck’ is an issue for artificial intelligence algorithms which require reading considerable amounts of data at each step, performing complicated operations on this data, and then writing the results back to memory. It slows down computing and considerably increases the energy consumption for learning and inference.” This further agrees with the point that, in order to advance the complexity and intelligence of AI, a biological approach to computing architecture is crucial to accompanying a similar processing level to the human brain. The importance of intelligent processing is therefore emphasised through the unique degree of neuromorphic computing, achieving biologically structured architecture through artificial brain cells.

How it will Revolutionise AI

Applications for AI now have the support to excel for advanced applications, not possible on conventional von Neumann architecture. Neuromorphic architecture enables smart applications to perform efficiently, exceeding the processing speed of AI tasks in comparison to conventional architecture.A recent collaboration between Accenture Labs and an automotive client revealed supporting evidence towards the performance of neuromorphic computing. It was demonstrated that a neuromorphic processor recognised voice commands up to 200 milliseconds faster than embedded GPU accelerators, with power usage of 1,000x less. Therefore, it can be stated that neuromorphic computers are power efficient, whilst producing faster compute results to conventional architecture.
